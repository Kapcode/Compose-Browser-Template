Search

With a search engine, you are entering in a few words and searching the entire internet for those words, trying to find the top documents and pages with those words on them, and hopefully they are the most relevant to what you want to learn about.

LLMs

LLMs you want to ask a question, and get an answer. Usually a detailed answer, with a few references to where the information came from. It's like a conversation with someone.
You are asking a mentor, or better yet a librarian‚Äîwho doesn‚Äôt already know the topic but is excellent at looking things up. If it‚Äôs not already part of their training, they look up what they can and explain it to you.
With LLMs they do the searching behind the curtains if the information is not within its training data, and it goes further than that. They try to explain the answer to the question you have, and help you solve your problem.

(Some things to note)

  • Not all LLMs provide their sources
  • Not all LLMs have access to the web
  • The user is always responsible for double checking the LLMs output

Examples: Search VS LLM

Problem 1:

Search: (apple android difference)

If I was using search to solve this problem I would not typically get good results by typing in the entire question into the search box.

I would use a few key words, and try to find the articles that best match what I'm looking for. Fewer words in your search term doesn't mean better, more doesn't mean better results either.

Search terms and results end up being just that, a query, and results of ranked pages based on if the terms are in those pages and how popular those pages are.

LLM:(what's the difference between apple and android?)

With LLM I would ask the question our right, and be as specific as possible, and where bias is concerned trying to be as neutral as possible. An LLM will handle the short search term just fine usually, but it might not, depending on the context of previous prompts.

An LLM prompt and answer becomes more like a conversation.

How can I improve my LMM prompt?

Add more detailed requests for information.

Ask for differences, and explanations of why they are different. What do you want to know exactly?
